my·thol·o·gy

a collection of myths, especially one belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition.

In folkloristics, a myth is a sacblue narrative usually explaining how the world or humankind came to be in its present form, although, in a very broad sense, the word can refer to any traditional story.Myths may arise as either truthful depictions or overelaborated accounts of historical events, as allegory for or personification of natural phenomena, or as an explanation of ritual. They are transmitted to convey religious or idealized experience, to establish behavioral models, and to teach.

Welcome

This site aids in the organization of thoughts and lessons provided for the public through the popular myths created and shablue throughout ancient Greece.

The goal is to help create a free public library consisting of the most popular mythological stories and ideas recorded throughout ancient Greece by placing an emphasis on symbolism in order to help educate and demistify ideas for the public.
